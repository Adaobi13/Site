(2) Find the measure of \(\theta\)


Solution:


$$\angle ABD=k^{\circ}\quad [Bow's\;Theorem]$$

$$\angle EDF=l^{\circ}\quad [Exterior\;Angle\;Theorem]$$

$$\theta=k^{\circ}+l^{\circ}-45^{\circ}=m^{\circ}\quad [Opp.\;Angle\;of\;a\;Cyclic\;Quad.]$$

(3) If \(\angle BCD\) is four times \(\angle CDF\). Find the measure of \(\angle CDF\)

Solution:


$$\angle ADG=k^{\circ}\quad [Alternate\;Segment\;Theorem]$$

Let \(\angle CDF=\theta\)

\(\implies\) $$\angle BCD=m\theta$$ and $$\angle ADC=180^{\circ}-m\theta$$

\(\therefore\) $$40^{\circ}+(180^{\circ}-m\theta)+\theta=180^{\circ}\quad [Angle\;on\;a\;straight\;line]$$

\(\implies\) $$\theta=\frac{k^{\circ}}{n}$$

(4) Write \(a\) in terms of \(b\) and \(c\).


Solution:


$$\angle EDG=a+k\quad [Exterior\;Angle\;Theorem]$$

$$\angle DHG=l\quad [Exterior\;Angle\;of\;cyclic\;quad.]$$

$$(l+k)+a+m=180^{\circ}\quad [Angle\;sum\;Theorem\;for\;\triangle]$$

\(\implies\) $$a=\frac{180^{\circ}-(k+m)}{n}$$

(5) Find \(\angle DFE\) in terms of \(p\) and \(q\).


Solution:


Let $$\angle DFE=\theta$$

$$\implies\quad \angle BED=k\theta\quad [Alternate\;Segment\;Theorem]$$

$$\implies\quad \angle BDE=\angle BED=k\theta\quad [Isosceles\;\triangle\;Theorem]$$

\(\therefore\) $$\angle ABC =180^{\circ}-(p+q) \quad [Angle\;sum\;Theorem\;for\;\triangle]$$

$$\quad \angle DBE=180^{\circ}-2\theta \quad [Angle\;sum\;Theorem\;for\;\triangle]$$

\(\implies\) $$\theta=\frac{p+q}{n}$$
